To whom it may concern,
(and all who are interested)

First off, thank you for taking the time to review my portfolio. I am a Cloud and Network Engineering student at Western Governors University, in the Amazon Web Services (AWS) track with a focus on becoming a cybersecurity professional. My passion lies in building secure, scalable systems and analyzing network threats.

My story begins working in telecommunications as a sales associate. It was there I could be close to technology; getting people connected to the network, learning to troubleshoot, and as an added bonus, being close to people. It was peoples' stories that lead me down this path. More specifically, the stories connected to people unfortunately being scammed or being affected by cyber incidents. At the time, I had also been hearing about the need for cybersecurity professionals. To keep a long story short, I dove head first into to world of cybersecurity, first starting with a cybersecurity course on Coursera and learning to code on FreeCodeCamp. Three years later, I'm pursing my degree, honing my skills on Hack The Box, and building projects in Python I never thought I could ever build.

// key_strengths

Web development (HTML, CSS, React, TypeScript, Python), automation scripting, security analysis, and hands-on experience with Docker-based lab environments.

Currently, I am developing a cybersecurity learning platform that combines educational content with interactive labs. The flagship feature is a SOC analyst simulation built with Docker, Elasticsearch, and a custom React SIEM dashboard. This project reflects my drive to merge development skills with security expertise.

I am eager to bring this blend of cloud engineering, web development, and security analysis to a team that values growth, curiosity, and hands-on problem-solving. I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my skills can contribute to your organization.

Thank you for your time,

Xavier Bodus
